Maxwell's fourth equation

The Ampère–Maxwell law ties magnetic circulation to current and to a changing electric field — the displacement-current term μ0ϵ0 ∂tE\mu_0 \epsilon_0 \,\partial_t \mathbf{E}μ0​ϵ0​∂t​E that Maxwell added to make the set consistent:

Making it concrete

A quick numerical check that c=1/μ0ϵ0c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}c=1/μ0​ϵ0​​ really lands on the speed of light:

import math

mu_0 = 4 * math.pi * 1e-7        # vacuum permeability  [T·m/A]
eps_0 = 8.8541878128e-12         # vacuum permittivity  [F/m]

c = 1.0 / math.sqrt(mu_0 * eps_0)
print(f"c = {c:.0f} m/s")        # -> c = 299792458 m/s

The same constants that govern a fridge magnet and a capacitor fix the speed of light to the meter-per-second. The inline form E=mc2E = mc^2E=mc2 then ties that speed to mass and energy — but that's a note for another day.
